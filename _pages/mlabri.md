Considered by some sensationalist sources to be "The last hunter-gatherers in Mainland South-East Asia", the Mlabri are an ethno-linguistic group living in scattered settlements across rural Northern Thailand. There is no good estimate on the population, though most sources land anywhere between 150-400 people. They blend traditional hunter-gathering and nomadic lifestyle strategies with working for other minoritiy ethnic groups in farms. I naively sought them out when I was a masters student, and have since routinely visited to live how they live, learn their language, and elicit data for research. 

Despite their kindness, the Mlabri are undergoing a humanitarian crisis. The gradual transition from fully nomadic hunter-gatherers to a semblence of what we call 'modern' life has been more than rough and has left them extremely vunerable to the dominant economic system. This has led to common cases of debt trapping and indentured servitude. Mass deforestation to make space for farmland has wiped out a significant portion of the forest that supplies the Mlabri with food and materials. Widespread poverty and malnutrition has led to social issues with alcohol and drug abuse.
