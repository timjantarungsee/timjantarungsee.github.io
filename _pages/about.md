I'm a Linguistics PhD Student at University College London supervised by Yasutada Sudo. Previously, I completed my MScR in Linguistics at The University of Edinburgh supervised by Wataru Uegaki and Anne Mucha. 

My core interests are formal semantics + pragmatics and endangered language documentation. On the theoretical side, I work mostly on formal explanatory theories for backgrounded meaning (presuppositions, conventional implicatures) and modality (ability modals, conditionals). On the fieldwork side, I work on endangered languages in East and South-East Asia, more specifically documenting and analysing semantic phenomena in Mlabri (Austro-Asiatic) and Yaeyaman (Ryukyuan). 

I'm also interested in other kind-of related stuff, such as philosophy of language, philosophical logic, decolonial linguistics, field methodology and sociocultural anthropology.
